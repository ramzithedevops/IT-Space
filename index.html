Solar System

The solar system consists of a star - the Sun and many objects that revolve around it. Includes eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. The system also contains dwarf planets such as Pluto, asteroids, comets and various satellites of planets. The Sun holds all these objects with its gravity, creating a system in which all objects interact and move in their orbits.


Orrery

An orrery is a model of the solar system that demonstrates the movements of planets around the Sun.
It typically includes scaled models of the planets that rotate around a central light (the Sun) according
to their real orbits and periods of revolution. Such devices can range from simple to complex, featuring
mechanisms that show not only the motion of the planets but also their phases and other astronomical
phenomena. Orreries are often used for educational purposes and in astronomy to better understand
the structure and dynamics of our solar system.

Since 1713, when a mechanical model of the solar system was presented to Charles Boyle, the 4th
Earl of Orrery, such models have been referred to as orreries. The first orreries were physical models,
but today we can use numerous tools to create virtual orreries that have many more features than their
ancient mechanical counterparts.


The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy that contains our Solar System.
It is approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter and is home to an estimated
100 to 400 billion stars. The galaxy has a central bulge surrounded by a flat
disk, which contains stars, gas, and dust, as well as several spiral arms.
The Milky Way is part of the Local Group of galaxies and is located about
26,000 light-years from the Galactic Center.
